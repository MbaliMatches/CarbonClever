What Is Carbon Footprint

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide, that a person, organization, or product releases into the environment. It comes from everyday activities like driving a car, using electricity, or even making products. The bigger the carbon footprint, the more harm it can cause to the environment by contributing to climate change.

Why Should You Care

People should care about their carbon footprint because it directly affects the health of our planet. A large carbon footprint contributes to climate change, which can lead to extreme weather, rising sea levels, and damage to ecosystems. By reducing our carbon footprint, we help protect the environment, ensure cleaner air and water, and create a healthier world for future generations.
